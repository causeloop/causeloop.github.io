Root cause

The mortgage underwriting model exhibits statistically significant disparate impact against a protected class — Hispanic applicants are denied at a 15% higher rate than baseline — and it cannot explain its decisions. This violates Fair Lending law (ECOA) and SR 11-7 model-risk management guidance.

The Cause

The model was deployed as a complex, uninterpretable deep neural network without adequate bias testing or explainability constraints during the validation phase.

Contributing factors

1. No disparate-impact / adverse-impact test gate in model validation.
2. Black-box architecture with no reason codes or explainability (e.g. SHAP).
3. Trained on historical lending data that carries embedded human bias, never debiased.
