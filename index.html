The problem

Thousands of "separate" issues are the same failure, repeating.

Issues live in silos and are treated as isolated events. So organizations keep fixing the same types of problems — never realizing they're connected.

Siloed by system

Risk, compliance, audit, ops and engineering each track issues their own way — no shared view across the business.

Manual root cause

Analysis is inconsistent and limited to the team that owns the ticket. Insight never travels.

Failures resurface

The same failure modes reappear in different parts of the org, quarter after quarter, disguised as new tickets.

About us

Born inside one enterprise. Built for all of them.

The short version of how we got here — and why fragmented issue data is a category-scale problem.

Causeloop began as a simplified internal system inside a large, systemically important institution. The team noticed something the dashboards never said out loud: most of what was being tracked as separate issues across the firm wasn't separate at all. The same underlying failure modes were recurring across business lines, year after year — hidden by org structure and the simple fact that no one had a view across the silos.

When those issues were connected — clustered by underlying pattern instead of by which team filed them — two things became obvious. First, the firm was solving the same problems repeatedly, paying for them every time. Second, once the patterns were visible, predicting where they'd recur next was straightforward, and remediation that had worked once could be propagated systematically.

That experience pointed to a category-scale opportunity. The same principle that worked inside one enterprise applies to every organization that drowns in fragmented issue data — which is, in our experience, most of them.
